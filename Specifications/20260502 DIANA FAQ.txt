Question
What is DIANA?
Answer
DIANA is the Defence Innovation Accelerator for the North Atlantic, a NATO organisation with a mission to find and accelerate dual-use innovation. Through an Alliance-wide network of innovators, accelerators, test centres and end-users, DIANA is solving critical defence, security and resilience challenges to support a more peaceful future.
Category
About NATO DIANA

Question
What is DIANA’s mission and vision?
Answer
DIANA envisions a secure and peaceful future where technology is integral to the governance, protection, prosperity and resilience of people around the world. Through a thriving innovation ecosystem that effectively integrates academia, industry, governments and not-for-profits, DIANA accelerates technology solutions across the full spectrum of conflict risk drivers, whether climate change, energy resilience, food insecurity, or warfare.

With 32 nations working together to create a collaborative, science-based and resilient technology pipeline, the world’s most successful Alliance can lead the way on innovation for peace and prosperity.

Authenticator app: I'm having issues receiving the one-time login code. Can you help?
Answer

Question
How can I add a colleague to my company for collaboration?
Answer
Firstly, the colleague should register as a user on the DIANA challenge portal, entering "Yes" to the question "Has a member of your company already joined the DIANA Challenges portal and created a company profile?"

Once they have registered, you may add their user to your company by selecting the "My Company" tab, then select 'Contacts' in the left menu, then select "Create new invitation".

Enter their registered email address, add a comment if you wish, and select "Send".

When next logging into the portal, your colleague will see an invitation to join your company which, if they select, will allow them to submit proposals to the DIANA Challenges from your company, or collaborate on existing proposals, with one proposal permitted per company per challenge.

If you are unable to view the 'Contacts button' in the left menu, it may be outside your visible screen. Please use the horizonal scroll bar to view it.

Question
How does the competitive selection process work?
Answer
Following an initial eligibility assessment, the competitive selection process proceeds in three steps.

Step 1 of the submission process involves evaluation of the ‘Short Form Proposal’.

Proposals that pass Step 1 will proceed to Step 2, which involves evaluation of the ‘Long Form Proposal’.

Proposals that pass Step 2 will proceed to Step 3, which takes the format of a virtual panel session, where innovators deliver their proposal through a presentation, followed by questions from the evaluation panel. Innovators that reach step 3 will receive guidance on how to prepare and participate.

Question
Is a proposal involving two sibling companies; two commonly owned companies; two companies with separate ownership; a consortium of companies; etc., permitted?
Answer
 
DIANA requires that there is one applicant comprised of one legal entity for purposes of submitting a proposal and the contractual relationship with DIANA if selected. However, it is common for applicants to rely on shared services among related companies (e.g. two companies under a common parent), and even in some cases for applicants to rely on IP or support from one or more third parties in the role of supplier, subcontractor, consortium participant, joint venture, or partnership. This is permitted and should be explained in the proposal to the extent relevant to the solution being proposed. Terms and conditions regarding eligibility including presence and incorporation in NATO, and the source of technology relied on for the proposal, would also extend to other companies participating alongside the applicant.

Which Technology Readiness Level ("TRL") should I provide in my proposal?
Answer
You should enter the current TRL (at the point of submission) of the technical solution you are proposing and would continue to iterate during the programme if selected.
 
We note the TRL of technical solutions may differ depending on the use case and/or operational environment. The TRL you enter must reflect the use case and/or operational environment you are proposing in your submission.

Question
Which technology readiness level (TRL) should I select for my proposal?
Answer
DIANA uses the following definitions for technology readiness levels (TRL):

TRL 1: Basic principles observed
TRL 2: Technology concept formulated
TRL 3: Experimental proof of concept
TRL 4: Technology validated in lab
TRL 5: Technology validated in relevant environment
TRL 6: Prototype demonstrated in relevant environment
TRL 7: Prototype demonstration in operational environment
TRL 8: Integrated system complete and operationally qualified
TRL 9: Integrated system is operational

These are based on the MIT Dual-Use Readiness Levels. 

We are unable to advise on specific cases regarding which TRL to use. Our recommendation is to consider the operational environment your proposal relates to.

Question
Who can submit a proposal? 
Answer
Any company with its principal place of business in a NATO member nation that is controlled and majority owned by nationals of NATO member nations is eligible to submit a proposal. The specific eligibility criteria are set out in the ‘Terms and Conditions of Bidding’.

DIANA’s challenges are targeted at start-ups or small and medium-sized enterprises with limited experience in defence and security.

DIANA will accept collaborative proposals as long as all member companies meet the eligibility requirements; a single company must be listed as the lead for the purposes of communication during the selection process, as well as for contracting and reporting requirements, should the proposal be selected.

Universities, public sector organisations and non-profit groups are not eligible to apply.

Question
Will my data and intellectual property be protected?
Answer
DIANA innovators retain all rights to their intellectual property. DIANA will neither take nor assert any rights over innovators’ intellectual property as part of the Challenge Programme, nor will any other stakeholders involved with the Challenge Programme. 

DIANA is committed to protecting innovator information and to fostering a trusted environment for innovators’ proprietary/confidential information and their intellectual property. All information submitted as part of a proposal is treated as strictly confidential and will only be accessible to the evaluators assigned to that proposal, all of whom are subject to strict confidentiality obligations.

Proposals are submitted via a secure portal, where each company receives a unique identifier and directions to create a unique login account. All information is encrypted in submission and in storage.

When in the programme innovators will receive guidance and mentoring and may also seek specialist support from DIANA’s Legal Connect network to provide additional levels of assurance on protection of data and intellectual property.
